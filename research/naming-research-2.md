# Naming Research - Round 2

## Summary

30+ name candidates for a vocabulary RPG app, researched across gaming, edtech, and multilingual etymology. Names prioritize 1-3 syllables, game-like personality, and logo potential. Organized by origin category with full analysis.

## What Makes Great Names Work

### RPG Game Names - Pattern Analysis

| Game | Syllables | Why It Works |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Hades | 2 | Mythological weight, single word, instant atmosphere |
| Celeste | 2 | Soft, aspirational, hints at climbing/reaching |
| Tunic | 2 | Concrete object, mysterious, implies adventure gear |
| Sable | 2 | Evocative color/texture, poetic, minimal |
| Elden Ring | 3 | Compound mystery - "elden" isn't a real word but feels ancient |
| Hollow Knight | 3 | Adjective + noun, creates visual identity |

**Patterns**: Short. Evocative over descriptive. Single mysterious words or tight two-word combos. Never explain the mechanic in the name. Sound > literal meaning.

### EdTech App Names - Pattern Analysis

| App | Strategy | Why It Works |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Duolingo | Portmanteau (duo + lingo) | Playful, says "language" without being boring |
| Brilliant | Single adjective | Aspirational - you become the name |
| Quizlet | Root + diminutive suffix | Friendly, approachable, implies small bites |
| Brainly | Noun + "-ly" suffix | Casual, modern, adverb energy |
| Habitica | Root + fantasy suffix | "Habit" + "-ica" = gamified habits, RPG feel |
| Anki | Japanese for "memorization" | Exotic, short, memorable, meaningful |

**Patterns**: Portmanteaus and neologisms dominate. Foreign words add mystique. Suffixes like -ly, -let, -ify make things feel app-like. Best names hint at the benefit, not the mechanic.

### Naming Strategy Matrix

| Strategy | Example | Strength | Risk |
|----------|---------|----------|------|
| Foreign word (real) | Anki | Depth, uniqueness | Pronunciation barrier |
| Neologism | Duolingo | Ownable, fresh | Meaningless if too abstract |
| Evocative single word | Brilliant, Celeste | Instant mood | Hard to trademark |
| Compound (adj+noun) | Hollow Knight | Visual, memorable | Can feel generic |
| Portmanteau | Habitica | Best of both roots | Can sound forced |

## Name Candidates

### Tier 1 - Top Picks (strongest overall)

#### 1. Kotodama
- **Origin**: Japanese 言霊 - "word spirit/soul." The belief that words hold mystical power.
- **Why it fits**: Literally about the magic of words. Perfect for an app where words gain power as you master them. The spiritual/mystical angle maps directly to RPG progression - words evolving, gaining strength.
- **Pronunciation**: koh-toh-DAH-mah (4 syllables, but flows naturally)
- **Vibe**: Game. Feels like a JRPG title. Think Okami, Katamari.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Japanese calligraphy + spirit/flame motif.

#### 2. Galdr
- **Origin**: Old Norse - "incantation, spell-song." Magic performed through spoken words.
- **Why it fits**: Words as spells. Vocabulary as power. Norse mythology ties into the RPG aesthetic perfectly. Odin learned galdr alongside runes.
- **Pronunciation**: GAHL-dr (1.5 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Dark fantasy RPG. Elden Ring energy.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Runic letterforms, angular, bold.

#### 3. Lexis
- **Origin**: Greek λέξις - "word, speech, diction." Root of "lexicon."
- **Why it fits**: Clean, short, directly about words. Sounds modern and sharp. Easy to say in any language.
- **Pronunciation**: LEK-sis (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App/game hybrid. Like a sleek tool with depth underneath.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Clean typography, the X gives visual punch.

#### 4. Verbora
- **Origin**: Neologism from Latin "verbum" (word) + "-ora" suffix (evokes "aurora," "flora").
- **Why it fits**: Sounds like a place you'd explore. "The world of words." Feminine, elegant, but not soft.
- **Pronunciation**: vehr-BOR-ah (3 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Fantasy world name. Could be a kingdom in an RPG.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Flowing letterforms, world-building aesthetic.

#### 5. Runelore
- **Origin**: Compound of Old Norse "rún" (rune/secret) + English "lore" (knowledge/tradition).
- **Why it fits**: Runes = letters/words. Lore = deep knowledge. Together: "the secret knowledge of words." Instantly communicates RPG + learning.
- **Pronunciation**: ROON-lor (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Pure RPG. Skyrim meets vocabulary.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Runic typography, ancient book motif.

#### 6. Codex
- **Origin**: Latin - "book, manuscript." Originally a bound book (vs. scroll). Also means "code."
- **Why it fits**: Dual meaning - ancient manuscript AND code/system. Implies collecting and organizing knowledge. Already has gaming associations (codex entries in RPGs).
- **Pronunciation**: KOH-deks (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App/game hybrid. Feels like a companion tool with depth.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Book spine, embossed lettering, leather texture.

#### 7. Shabda
- **Origin**: Sanskrit शब्द - "word, sound, speech." The divine essence of language itself.
- **Why it fits**: Beautiful sound. Means literally "word" but carries spiritual weight. In Hindu philosophy, shabda is a path to understanding reality.
- **Pronunciation**: SHAHB-dah (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Exotic, mysterious. Like a meditation RPG.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Devanagari-inspired letterforms, sound wave motif.

#### 8. Kavi
- **Origin**: Sanskrit कवि - "poet, sage, wise one."
- **Why it fits**: Short, punchy, beautiful. You're becoming a kavi - a word-sage. Aspirational without being pretentious.
- **Pronunciation**: KAH-vee (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Clean, modern, Duolingo-tier approachability.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Minimal, works at any size. Strong icon potential.

### Tier 2 - Strong Contenders

#### 9. Wordgaldr
- **Origin**: Compound of English "word" + Old Norse "galdr" (spell-song).
- **Why it fits**: "Word-magic." Explicit about what you're doing (words) with RPG flavor (spells).
- **Pronunciation**: WORD-gahl-dr (2.5 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Norse fantasy.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Runic + modern hybrid.

#### 10. Gnosis
- **Origin**: Greek γνῶσις - "knowledge, insight." Deep intuitive understanding, not just facts.
- **Why it fits**: Implies mastery beyond memorization. Has mystical/esoteric associations. Already used in gaming contexts.
- **Pronunciation**: NOH-sis (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game/tool hybrid. Intellectual but not boring.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Eye/knowledge symbolism.

#### 11. Futhark
- **Origin**: The runic alphabet (named after its first six letters: F-U-Th-A-R-K).
- **Why it fits**: Literally an alphabet system. Runes = letters = words. Deep Norse mythology connection (Odin's sacrifice).
- **Pronunciation**: FOO-thark (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Viking/Norse RPG.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Runic letterforms are inherently iconic.

#### 12. Vidya
- **Origin**: Sanskrit विद्या - "knowledge, learning, science." Same root as Latin "video" (I see).
- **Why it fits**: Beautiful word. Means knowledge/learning but sounds like a character name. Also internet slang for "video games" (vidya gaems).
- **Pronunciation**: VID-yah (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App/game. Approachable, warm.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Flowing script, eye/light motif.

#### 13. Ordhord
- **Origin**: Old English "wordhord" - "word-hoard." A poet's vocabulary was their treasure chest of words.
- **Why it fits**: PERFECT metaphor. Your vocabulary is literally a hoard of word-treasure. Collecting words = collecting loot.
- **Pronunciation**: ORD-hord (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Anglo-Saxon/Tolkien energy.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Treasure chest + runes.

#### 14. Lectio
- **Origin**: Latin - "a reading, a choosing." Root of "lecture" and "lesson."
- **Why it fits**: About reading AND choosing (selecting words). Elegant, academic but not stuffy.
- **Pronunciation**: LEK-tee-oh (3 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Polished, premium feel.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Serif typography, book motif.

#### 15. Bodhi
- **Origin**: Sanskrit बोधि - "awakening, enlightenment." The Buddha's moment of understanding.
- **Why it fits**: Learning as awakening. Each word mastered is a small enlightenment. Aspirational.
- **Pronunciation**: BOH-dee (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Calm, mindful, but with depth.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Bodhi tree leaf, minimal.

#### 16. Staves
- **Origin**: Plural of "stave" - a rune-letter in Norse tradition. Also a musical staff.
- **Why it fits**: Letters/runes as collectible items. Musical connotation adds rhythm. Short and punchy.
- **Pronunciation**: STAYVZ (1 syllable)
- **Vibe**: Game. Indie game energy. Like Tunic or Sable.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Vertical lines, runic, minimal.

### Tier 3 - Solid Options

- **Origin**: Latin - "words" (plural of verbum).
- **Why it fits**: Direct, clean, about words. Sounds like a character or place name.
- **Pronunciation**: VEHR-bah (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Modern, minimal.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Clean sans-serif.

#### 18. Prajna
- **Origin**: Sanskrit प्रज्ञा - "wisdom, deep insight, transcendent understanding."
- **Why it fits**: The highest form of knowledge in Buddhist philosophy. Implies going beyond surface learning.
- **Pronunciation**: PRAHJ-nyah (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App/tool. Contemplative, premium.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Lotus or mandala motif.

#### 19. Saga
- **Origin**: Old Norse - "story, tale, narrative." Also common English word.
- **Why it fits**: Your reading journey IS a saga. Each book is a chapter. Universal recognition.
- **Pronunciation**: SAH-gah (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game/app. Broadly appealing. Maybe too common.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. But may conflict with existing brands.

#### 20. Chie
- **Origin**: Japanese 知恵 - "wisdom, intelligence." Practical, applied knowledge.
- **Why it fits**: Short, sweet, means wisdom. Sounds like a friendly companion character.
- **Pronunciation**: CHEE-eh (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Cute, approachable, Duolingo-adjacent.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Owl or fox mascot potential.

#### 21. Liber
- **Origin**: Latin - "book" AND "free." Dual meaning.
- **Why it fits**: Books that set you free. Knowledge as liberation. Two meanings in one word.
- **Pronunciation**: LEE-behr (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App/tool. Intellectual, clean.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Open book + wings/freedom motif.

#### 22. Dokusho
- **Origin**: Japanese 読書 - "reading" (lit. "read-book").
- **Why it fits**: Directly about reading. Sounds exotic to English speakers. Clean compound.
- **Pronunciation**: DOH-koo-shoh (3 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Japanese aesthetic, minimalist.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Kanji-inspired mark.

### Tier 4 - Compound Words & Neologisms

#### 23. Lexborne
- **Origin**: "Lex" (Greek: word) + "borne" (carried, as in "airborne").
- **Why it fits**: "Carried by words" or "word-born." Sounds like a fantasy class or origin story.
- **Pronunciation**: LEKS-born (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Dark fantasy. Bloodborne energy.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Gothic typography.

#### 24. Grimoire
- **Origin**: French/Medieval - a book of magic spells and invocations.
- **Why it fits**: Your word collection IS a grimoire - a book of power. Already has RPG associations.
- **Pronunciation**: grim-WAHR (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Dark academia meets RPG.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Spellbook, arcane symbols.

#### 25. Wordwild
- **Origin**: Compound of "word" + "wild" (untamed, natural).
- **Why it fits**: Words as wild creatures to discover and tame. Exploration + collection.
- **Pronunciation**: WORD-wyld (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Adventure/exploration. Breath of the Wild energy.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Nature + typography hybrid.

#### 26. Lexica
- **Origin**: From Greek "lexikos" (of words). Plural/place form.
- **Why it fits**: Sounds like a realm or world. "Welcome to Lexica." Clean, recognizable root.
- **Pronunciation**: LEK-sih-kah (3 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game/app hybrid. Fantasy world.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Map/world motif.

#### 27. Runevault
- **Origin**: "Rune" (Norse: letter/secret) + "vault" (secure collection).
- **Why it fits**: Your word collection stored in a vault. Runes = ancient letters. Treasure + knowledge.
- **Pronunciation**: ROON-vawlt (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Dungeon crawler meets library.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Vault door with runic inscriptions.

#### 28. Verbquest
- **Origin**: Latin "verbum" (word) + "quest" (journey/mission).
- **Why it fits**: Direct - you're on a quest for words. Clear value proposition in the name.
- **Pronunciation**: VERB-kwest (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Classic RPG quest structure.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Compass + scroll.

#### 29. Logoseed
- **Origin**: Greek "logos" (word/reason) + "seed" (growth, potential).
- **Why it fits**: Words as seeds that grow into knowledge. Maps to the word evolution mechanic.
- **Pronunciation**: LOH-goh-seed (3 syllables)
- **Vibe**: App. Growth/garden metaphor.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Sprouting seed with letters.

#### 30. Inkborn
- **Origin**: "Ink" (writing medium) + "born" (origin, creation).
- **Why it fits**: Born from ink/reading. Sounds like a character class. "You are Inkborn."
- **Pronunciation**: INK-born (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Fantasy RPG class name.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Ink splatter + character silhouette.

#### 31. Glyphic
- **Origin**: From Greek "glyphe" (carving). Related to hieroglyphics.
- **Why it fits**: Words as carved symbols of power. Ancient + modern. Implies visual evolution of words.
- **Pronunciation**: GLIF-ik (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game/app. Mysterious, archaeological.
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Carved stone, symbol marks.

#### 32. Lexsoul
- **Origin**: "Lex" (Greek: word) + "soul" (spirit, essence).
- **Why it fits**: The soul of words. Direct parallel to Kotodama's meaning in English-friendly form.
- **Pronunciation**: LEK-sole (2 syllables)
- **Vibe**: Game. Indie RPG. Dark Souls adjacent naming.
- **Logo potential**: Good. Flame/spirit + letter motif.

#### 33. Tome
- **Origin**: Greek "tomos" - a volume, a cut section of a larger work.
- **Why it fits**: Single word. Universally understood as "big important book." Short, punchy, iconic.
- **Pronunciation**: TOHM (1 syllable)
- **Vibe**: Game/app. Minimal, powerful.
- **Logo potential**: Excellent. Book icon, works at any size.

#### 34. Nám
- **Origin**: Old Norse - "learning, study." From the same word Odin used when he "took up" the runes.
- **Why it fits**: Odin's word for learning. One syllable. Mythological weight in a tiny package.
- **Pronunciation**: NAHM (1 syllable)
- **Vibe**: App. Minimal, mysterious. Like "Calm" or "Notion."
- **Logo potential**: Strong. Runic letterform, accent mark adds visual interest.

## Recommendations

### If you want maximum RPG energy:
**Galdr**, **Runelore**, **Grimoire**, **Lexborne**, **Inkborn**

### If you want broad app-store appeal:
**Kavi**, **Lexis**, **Codex**, **Nám**, **Tome**

### If you want something truly unique and meaningful:
**Kotodama**, **Shabda**, **Verbora**, **Galdr**, **Ordhord**

### If you want the best logo potential:
**Galdr**, **Staves**, **Grimoire**, **Kavi**, **Futhark**

### My top 5 overall (balancing all factors):
1. **Kotodama** - The meaning is too perfect. "Word spirit" for a word RPG. JRPG aesthetic. Memorable.
2. **Galdr** - Norse word-magic. Short, sharp, unique. Incredible logo potential.
3. **Kavi** - Clean, short, means "poet-sage." Best app-store name of the bunch.
4. **Grimoire** - Everyone knows what a grimoire is. Spellbook = word collection. Instant recognition.
5. **Verbora** - Original, ownable, sounds like a world to explore. Latin roots give it weight.
