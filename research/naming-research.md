# Naming Research: Reading RPG Vocabulary App

## Executive Summary

This document analyzes naming strategies from 7 successful apps, then generates 35+ name candidates across 5 categories. The goal: find a name that captures the RPG/game feel, reading companion aspect, and word collection/mastery journey - something that sounds interesting and memorable, not like a boring educational tool.

The strongest candidates from this research: **Grimoire**, **Logosync**, **Glyphborn**, **Runelore**, **Verbora**, **Athanor**, and **Lexis**.

---

## Part 1: How Successful Apps Got Their Names

### Naming Pattern Analysis

| App | Name Origin | Strategy | Why It Works |
|-----|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Duolingo** | Latin *duo* (two) + *lingo* (language) | Portmanteau of core concept | Instantly communicates "two languages" while sounding playful and unique |
| **Anki** | Japanese 暗記 (anki) = "memorization" | Foreign word for core function | Short, exotic, memorable. The foreignness adds mystique. Users learn the meaning and it sticks. |
| **Kindle** | Old Norse *kyndill* (candle); English "to kindle" = ignite | Metaphor for the experience | Evokes lighting a fire of knowledge/excitement. Branding consultants chose it because Bezos wanted to "talk about the future of reading in a small, not braggadocio way." |
| **Notion** | English "notion" = an idea, a concept | Abstract concept word | Simple, elegant, suggests the birth of ideas. Works because the product is about capturing thoughts. |
| **Obsidian** | Volcanic glass - hard, dark, sharp | Material metaphor | Suggests durability (local files), sharpness (precision), and depth. The mineral metaphor implies something natural and enduring. |
| **Forest** | English "forest" = a growing ecosystem | Growth metaphor | You plant trees by staying focused. The name IS the mechanic. Simple, visual, universal. |
| **Habitica** | *Habit* + *-ica* (suffix suggesting a place/land) | Portmanteau of core concept + fantasy suffix | Originally "HabitRPG" (too literal, hard to pronounce). Renamed to Habitica - the fantasy land where productivity adventures happen. The *-ica* suffix makes it sound like a place you visit. |

### Key Naming Lessons

1. **Portmanteaus work when both halves are instantly recognizable** (Duolingo, Habitica)
2. **Foreign words work when they're short and phonetically pleasing** (Anki)
3. **Metaphors work when the name IS the experience** (Kindle, Forest)
4. **Material/object names work when they evoke the right qualities** (Obsidian)
5. **Abstract concept words work when they're already in common use** (Notion)
6. **Avoid being too literal** - HabitRPG was renamed because it was confusing and hard to say
7. **2-3 syllables is the sweet spot** - every successful app above is 2-3 syllables
8. **The name should spark curiosity** - "Why is it called that?" is a feature, not a bug

### Naming Strategy Matrix for This App

The app's core identity pillars:
- **Reading** (books, chapters, pages)
- **RPG/Game** (XP, levels, bosses, skill trees, rarity tiers)
- **Words/Vocabulary** (collection, mastery, evolution, morphemes)
- **Journey/Quest** (progression, unlocking, discovery)

The name should lean into 1-2 of these pillars, not try to capture all 4.

## Part 4: Top 10 Shortlist with Deep Analysis

### Tier 1: Strongest Candidates

#### 1. Grimoire
- **What it means**: A book of magic spells (from Old French *grammaire* - grammar)
- **Why it's perfect**: The entire app IS a grimoire. You collect word-spells from books, they have rarity tiers, you master them through practice. The etymology connecting to "grammar" is a hidden bonus. Fantasy players instantly get it. Non-gamers find it intriguing.
- **Pronunciation**: Easy - GRIM-wahr. 2 syllables.
- **Risks**: Some existing uses in gaming. The .com is taken. But grimoire.app, getgrimoire.com, or mygrimoire.com are likely available.
- **App Store appeal**: High. Sounds magical, mysterious, bookish. Would pair well with a spellbook-themed UI.

#### 2. Glyphborn
- **What it means**: Glyphs (symbolic characters) that are born/created as you discover them
- **Why it's perfect**: Captures the word evolution mechanic (words are "born" then evolve). Sounds like a fantasy race or character class. Novel enough to own completely.
- **Pronunciation**: Easy - GLIF-born. 2 syllables.
- **Risks**: Might sound too "hardcore gamer" for casual readers.
- **App Store appeal**: High for the RPG crowd. The "-born" suffix (Skyrim's Dragonborn, Bloodborne) triggers instant game recognition.

#### 3. Verbora
- **What it means**: Latin *verbum* (word) + exploratory suffix. A world of words.
- **Why it's perfect**: Sounds like a place - "Welcome to Verbora." Elegant, feminine, approachable. Works for both gamers and readers. Easy to brand around.
- **Pronunciation**: Easy - ver-BOR-ah. 3 syllables.
- **Risks**: Might sound too soft/pretty for the RPG crowd.
- **App Store appeal**: High. Sounds like a fantasy world name. Unique and ownable.

#### 4. Wordborne
- **What it means**: Words carried/born from reading
- **Why it's perfect**: Instant game energy (Bloodborne, Airborne). Communicates the core mechanic: words are born from your reading and carried with you. Simple, strong, memorable.
- **Pronunciation**: Easy - WURD-born. 2 syllables.
- **Risks**: Could be confused with "Wordborn" (one letter difference from Glyphborn above).
- **App Store appeal**: Very high. Sounds like a AAA game title.

#### 5. Runelore
- **What it means**: Runes (magical knowledge symbols) + lore (accumulated wisdom)
- **Why it's perfect**: Odin sacrificed his eye for runic wisdom. You sacrifice time (reading) for word-wisdom. Each word is a rune you unlock. The Norse mythology connection is rich and resonant.
- **Pronunciation**: Easy - ROON-lor. 2 syllables.
- **Risks**: Might feel too Norse/Viking-specific for a reading app.
- **App Store appeal**: High for fantasy fans. Might confuse non-gamers.

### Tier 2: Strong Alternatives

#### 6. Athanor
- **What it means**: The alchemist's furnace where transmutation happens
- **Why it's perfect**: Directly ties to the morpheme alchemy feature. Reading is the furnace, words are the raw material, mastery is the gold. Obscure enough to fully own.
- **Pronunciation**: ATH-uh-nor. 3 syllables. Slightly harder.
- **App Store appeal**: Medium. Intriguing but unfamiliar.

#### 7. Morphica
- **What it means**: Morpheme + *-ica* (fantasy land suffix, like Habitica)
- **Why it's perfect**: Follows the proven Habitica naming pattern. The land of morphemes. Directly references the morpheme alchemy feature.
- **Pronunciation**: MOR-fih-kah. 3 syllables.
- **App Store appeal**: Medium-High. The Habitica parallel helps.

#### 8. Lorecraft
- **What it means**: Crafting lore/knowledge
- **Why it's perfect**: Warcraft, Minecraft, Starcraft - the "-craft" suffix is gaming royalty. "Lore" = knowledge/stories. You craft knowledge from reading.
- **Pronunciation**: LOR-kraft. 2 syllables.
- **App Store appeal**: Very high. Instant game recognition.

#### 9. Kotodama
- **What it means**: Japanese 言霊 - "word spirit/soul"
- **Why it's perfect**: The belief that words carry spiritual power. In the app, words literally have power (rarity, XP, evolution). Follows the Anki strategy of using a Japanese word. Beautiful and meaningful.
- **Pronunciation**: koh-toh-DAH-mah. 4 syllables. Harder for English speakers.
- **App Store appeal**: Medium. Exotic and intriguing but harder to remember/spell.

#### 10. Stardex
- **What it means**: Star + dex (index/Pokedex)
- **Why it's perfect**: Your word collection as a star catalog. Rarity tiers = star brightness. The "dex" suffix instantly signals "collection game" to Pokemon fans. Short, punchy, modern.
- **Pronunciation**: STAR-dex. 2 syllables.
- **App Store appeal**: High. Sounds like a game. Easy to say and remember.

## Part 5: Naming Decision Framework

When choosing, score each candidate on:

| Criterion | Weight | Question |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Memorability** | 25% | Can someone remember it after hearing it once? |
| **Speakability** | 15% | Can you say it easily in conversation? "Have you tried ___?" |
| **Game feel** | 20% | Does it sound like something you'd download from a game store? |
| **Reading connection** | 15% | Does it connect to books/reading/words? |
| **Ownability** | 15% | Can you own the domain, GitHub, social handles? |
| **Curiosity factor** | 10% | Does it make someone ask "What's that?" |

## Part 6: Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Grimoire**

It's the only name that perfectly captures ALL four identity pillars without being a forced portmanteau:
- Reading: A grimoire IS a book
- RPG/Game: Grimoires are core fantasy RPG items
- Words: The etymology literally comes from "grammar"
- Journey: You fill your grimoire as you progress

It passes the "coffee shop test" - "I've been using Grimoire to track my vocabulary" sounds natural and intriguing.

**Runner-up: Wordborne**

If you want maximum game energy and instant clarity about what the app does, Wordborne is the strongest. It's the most "app store optimized" name - someone browsing would immediately understand it's about words + adventure.

**Dark horse: Lorecraft**

If the crafting/alchemy angle is central to the brand identity, Lorecraft leverages the most powerful suffix in gaming history (-craft) while keeping the knowledge/reading connection through "lore."
